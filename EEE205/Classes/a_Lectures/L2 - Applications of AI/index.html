    Definitions of AI¶

    "AI is concerned with intelligent behaviour in artefacts. Intelligent behaviour in turn involves perception, reasoning, learning, communicating and acting in complex environments."

    Artificial Intelligence a New Synthesis, Nils Nilsson.

    A Traditional Definition of AI¶

    Russell and Norvig in their seminal textbook AI: A Modern Approach express AI in terms of four dimensions:

    1. Thinking humanly,
    2. Thinking rationally,
    3. Acting humanly, and
    4. Acting rationally.

    A Game AI?¶

    Ian Millington in “AI for Games” defines AI as:

    “making computers able to perform the thinking tasks that humans and animals are capable of”.

    He distinguishes academic AI from game AI.

    What is intelligence?¶

    “Human intelligence is a mental quality that consists of the abilities to learn from experience, adapt to new situations, understand and handle abstract concepts, and use knowledge to manipulate one's environment.”

    Consciousness?¶

    Consciousness, at its simplest, is sentience or awareness of internal and external existence. [^1]

    Types of AI¶

    • Weak AI: typically focused on narrow tasks, e.g. controlling flow in a pipe.
    • Strong AI: AI that can adapt, problem solve or learn.
    • Artificial General Intelligence (AGI): more human-like in being able to generalise well be able to perform a range of different tasks.
    • Superintelligence: better than human – faster, more accurate, beyond human understanding.
